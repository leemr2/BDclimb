## Your crux success rate arc

This is the number that matters most. Look at your week-by-week crux-after-fatigue success rate from baseline to now. If it has climbed, the training is transferring — the strength, base, and threshold work are showing up where it counts. If it has stalled, that is useful information too, and the next section helps explain why.

## Your fluency stop trend

Compare your stops-per-set from your first ARC sessions to your recent ones. A meaningful reduction is direct evidence of improved movement economy — fewer hesitations, fewer pump spikes, smoother passage to the rests. This is one of the few skill adaptations you can watch happen as a number over eight weeks.

## Where improvement is coming from

- If your **crux rate improved**, the threshold work is doing its job — your forearms are arriving at the crux with more in reserve.
- If your **crux rate stalled but IHE reps and max hang rose**, your capacity built but the specific crux skill needs more volume — Mesocycle 3 supplies exactly that.
- If your **fluency stops fell sharply**, expect pacing on real routes to feel noticeably easier even where raw numbers moved less.

## Mesocycle 3 preview

The final phase shifts from building capacity to practicing the actual skill under competition-like conditions. Your goal-route crux moves into the center of the work, and route-linking burns rehearse the real thing. Use this deload to recover fully — the next three weeks are where the training becomes performance.
