## What a taper is for

The fitness is already built — you cannot add meaningful capacity in the final week, but you can absolutely arrive fresh enough to express it. The taper sheds accumulated fatigue while preserving neuromuscular sharpness, so the engine you built over 11 weeks runs at full output when it matters.

## The volume-intensity rule

Cut total volume significantly, but keep some intensity. A few crisp, high-quality efforts — short crux rehearsals, a couple of near-limit hangs — remind your nervous system how to fire without digging a fatigue hole. Long, grinding sessions in the final week only cost you; they cannot bank anything.

## Expect to feel under-worked

The most common taper mistake is panicking at feeling fresh and adding "just one more hard session." Feeling slightly under-worked is the goal, not a warning sign. The restlessness is your fatigue clearing. Trust it.

## On performance day

Warm up thoroughly and unhurried, climb decisively, and use the pacing and rests you rehearsed in Mesocycle 3. Your Week 12 assessment captures where you finished — but the real test is the route. Everything in the program pointed here: arrive at the crux less pumped than you ever have, and execute.
