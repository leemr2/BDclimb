## Start with the crux rate

Your crux-after-fatigue success rate is the number this whole program was built to move. Compare your Week 12 score to your Week 0 baseline. That change — more than any single hang or rep count — tells you how much better you now execute hard moves when already pumped, which is the exact demand of your goal routes.

## Read the four assessments together

Lay your Week 0, 4, 8, and 12 results side by side. The shape of each line tells a story:

- **Max hang** rising steadily means your strength ceiling kept climbing and supported everything above it.
- **Intermittent endurance reps** rising shows your forearm capacity grew — the base and threshold work landed.
- **Fluency stops and silent-foot slips** falling is your movement economy improving, which shows up as better pacing on real routes.
- **Shoulder symptom score** staying low confirms the antagonist work protected you through a high-load block.

## Where your gains came from

If your crux rate jumped alongside endurance and fluency, the full chain transferred. If max hang rose but the crux rate lagged, you have strength to convert — a future block can lean harder on threshold and specificity. If fluency improved most, technique was your unlock, and continued movement work will keep paying off.

## What comes next

You have options: repeat the cycle with higher targets, switch goals to address a different limiter (pure strength via the bouldering program, for example), or take a planned lighter block to consolidate before the next push. Whatever you choose, you now have a full cycle of honest data to calibrate it — the most valuable thing you built besides the fitness itself.
