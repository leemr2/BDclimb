## From capacity to performance

For eight weeks you built the engine: aerobic base, anaerobic capacity, and a higher forearm threshold. Mesocycle 3 spends it. The work shifts toward route-specific practice — linking sections, dialing beta, and rehearsing the exact demands of your goal route under realistic fatigue.

## Why redpoint linking is structured

Redpoint progress is not just fitness; it is tactics. The route-practice sessions are run burn-by-burn so you can separate the variables that actually decide a send: pacing through the sustained section, finding and using rests, executing the crux when pumped, and committing on the moves after the crux. Logging each burn — high point, pump at the crux, where you fall consistently — turns flailing into a plan.

## The fluency constraint toggle

On rehearsal burns, keep the fluency constraint on: move efficiently, count your stops, and refine the beta. On genuine send attempts, turn it off — a real redpoint is about commitment and reading the moment, not about hitting a stop count. The app lets you toggle this per burn so the data stays clean.

## What to watch

Your crux success rate should now reflect everything upstream. If you are failing consistently in the same place, the bottleneck field tells you what to address — finger strength, power-endurance, pacing, rest inefficiency, or beta that is not yet dialed. Keep weekly load in check: this phase is about quality attempts, not volume.
