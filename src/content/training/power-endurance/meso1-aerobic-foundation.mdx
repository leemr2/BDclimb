## What your assessment told us

Your Week 0 results gave us two anchors: a crux-after-fatigue baseline and a max hang number. The crux score is the target we are chasing for 12 weeks; the max hang is one of the upstream inputs that should push it higher. Mesocycle 1 builds the foundation both of those depend on.

## The power-endurance problem

The limiting factor on a sustained route with a late crux is rarely the crux moves in isolation. It is doing those moves when you are already significantly fatigued. That means the most valuable thing we can change early is not raw strength — it is how pumped you are *when you arrive* at the hard section.

## Why aerobic base first

A stronger aerobic base means your forearms clear metabolic byproducts faster between moves and at rests. The practical effect is large: you can arrive at the crux meaningfully less pumped — not 10% less, potentially 30–40% less. We build that capacity in Mesocycle 1 with low-intensity continuous climbing before we stress the anaerobic system in Mesocycle 2.

## What silent feet and fluency actually do

The ARC sessions carry two constraints — silent feet and fluency. These are not just form drills. Every foot scrape and every hesitation longer than two seconds is a small energy leak, and across 60–100 moves on a route those leaks add up to the extra pump that makes you fall on the crux. We count slips and stops so the improvement is measurable, not a vague "felt smoother today."

## Your Week 1-3 targets

- **ARC sessions** stay at RPE 4–6. If you are getting pumped, you are going too hard and undermining the session's purpose.
- **Max hangs** build toward roughly 85–90% of your tested max with full recovery between efforts.
- **The crux simulation** at the end of your specificity session is your weekly KPI — log it every time, including the rounds you fail.
