## What silent feet actually does

Every foot scrape is wasted energy and a small loss of precision. On a route you will make 60–100 foot placements, and each sloppy one forces a micro-correction somewhere up the chain — a re-grip, a body-tension spike, a wasted breath. Silent feet — placing each foot precisely, once, with no scraping — removes those leaks. The rule is simple: every placement is silent and one-and-done.

## What fluency does

Fluency means not stopping for more than two seconds unless you are at a designated rest hold. Every unplanned stop is a pump spike: standing still on small holds while you figure out the next move costs you more than just time — it is a partial pump ratchet that you cannot fully recover from mid-route. Training fluency builds the movement reading that keeps you moving smoothly toward the rests.

## Why we track the numbers

Without a number, "pretty good feet today" means nothing across 12 weeks. We count slips and stops every set so the trend is objective. A downward trend in both is one of the clearest signals that your movement economy is improving — and that improvement shows up directly as better pacing and less pump on your goal routes.

## What to expect this week

High stop and slip counts early are completely normal — this is a new constraint and your attention is divided. The goal is not to be perfect immediately; it is to reduce the counts week over week. Watch the trend, not any single set.
