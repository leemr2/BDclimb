## Reading final numbers

Compare assessments from Weeks 0, 4, 8, and 12 as a timeline — not isolated snapshots. Max hang shows finger strength progression; campus reach tracks power development; benchmark send rate reflects on-the-wall transfer.

## What improved

Celebrate measurable gains even if they feel small. A 5% max hang increase across 12 weeks is meaningful for tendons. Send-rate improvements show training carried over to real climbing. Note which mesocycle produced the biggest jump — that informs future programming.

## Retest vs send rate

Strength can improve while send rate plateaus if limit attempts were inconsistent or if problem selection didn't match your strengths. Conversely, send rate may rise when movement efficiency improved even with modest hang numbers. Read both metrics together.

## What's next

You can repeat the bouldering cycle with updated baselines, switch to a route-focused program, or take an unstructured climbing block to apply gains. Save your Week 12 data — it's the starting reference for whatever you choose next.
