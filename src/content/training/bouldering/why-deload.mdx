## You're not getting weaker

Muscles and tendons don't get stronger during hard training — they adapt during recovery. Three weeks of progressive max hangs and limit bouldering created stimulus; this week lets your body consolidate those gains before the next block.

## The injury equation

Climbing overuse injuries, especially finger pulley strains, are almost always load-management problems. Deload weeks are your insurance policy: reduced volume with maintained intensity keeps tissues resilient without accumulating fatigue.

## What deload looks like

Session volume drops roughly 40–50%. You still touch intensity — some hard hangs and limit attempts — but total work is lower. You'll retest max hang and revisit benchmark boulders to measure how much you've improved since Week 0.

## Reading your retest results

A small gain on max hang is a win. Flat results after a hard block may mean recovery wasn't complete or loads progressed too fast — both are useful data. Send rate on benchmarks shows whether strength gains are transferring to the wall.

## What's coming in Mesocycle 2

You've built the foundation. Next up: power and rate-of-force development — campus board work and explosive limit bouldering. That phase needs fresh fingers and a confirmed strength base, which is exactly what this deload prepares you for.
