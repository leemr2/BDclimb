## High reward, high risk

Campus board training is one of the most effective tools for developing finger power — and one of the fastest routes to overuse injury if misused. Treat every campus session with respect: quality contacts over quantity.

## Prerequisites

You should have a solid max hang base before adding campus work. If Week 0 or Week 4 max hang results were low relative to bodyweight, prioritize hangboard strength before increasing dynamic load. Control on large rungs matters more than reaching the highest rung.

## Pain rules

Stop immediately on sharp pain, audible pops, or pain that persists into the next day. Fatigue and mild pump are expected; joint-line pain is not. When in doubt, skip the set and log how your fingers feel in the morning check-in.

## When to skip

Skip campus entirely if your finger status is elevated in the daily check-in, if you're returning from any finger injury, or if sleep and energy are poor. A missed campus session never outweighs a month lost to injury.
