## Why volume drops

Week 12 is a taper — not a rest week, not a deload identical to Weeks 4 and 8. Total training volume drops sharply so accumulated fatigue clears while your nervous system stays sharp for final assessments and limit attempts.

## Keeping intensity

You still touch heavy loads and hard moves, but with fewer sets and longer rest. The goal is to feel springy, not flat. If sessions feel too easy, that's often a sign the taper is working — resist the urge to add extra volume.

## Pre-performance mindset

Many climbers feel antsy during taper weeks. Trust the process: fitness won't disappear in a few days of reduced work. Use extra time for sleep, mobility, and mental rehearsal of benchmark problems rather than sneaking in extra climbing.
