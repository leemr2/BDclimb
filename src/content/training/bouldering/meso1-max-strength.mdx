## What you just learned from your assessment

Your max hang result tells us how much total load you can hold for seven seconds on a standard edge. As a percentage of bodyweight, this places you on the strength spectrum and determines starting loads for hangboard work.

## Why max strength comes first

Think of finger strength as your ceiling. Every other quality — power, endurance, efficiency — sits below it. If the ceiling is low, harder moves cost more and fatigue accumulates faster. Building strength first raises that ceiling so later phases have a solid base.

## What the next 3 weeks look like

You'll complete two max hang sessions per week plus limit bouldering. Loads increase conservatively — typically 2–3% when every set is clean and recovery is good. Limit boulder sessions stay focused on hard attempts with adequate rest between tries.

## What to watch for

Finger tenderness after sessions is normal. Sharp pain, especially on the front of the finger or palm-side of joints, is a signal to stop and reassess. Distinguish productive fatigue from warning signs early — it protects your tendons through three weeks of progressive loading.

## Your targets

By Week 4 retest, aim for a 5–10% improvement on max hang load. That might feel modest, but consistent small gains across a full cycle compound into meaningful progress. Your retest at the deload week confirms whether the foundation is in place for power work.
