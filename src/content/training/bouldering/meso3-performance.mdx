## Transfer to climbing

Mesocycles 1 and 2 built strength and power in isolated and semi-isolated formats. Mesocycle 3 asks: can you use that on real boulders? Sessions emphasize limit attempts, benchmark reattempts, and movement that mirrors your goals.

## Limit attempts and benchmarks

Quality over quantity. Fewer attempts with full rest beat grinding dozens of tries. Revisit benchmark problems from Week 0 to measure send-rate improvement — that's often the metric climbers care about most.

## Maintaining gains

You won't abandon hangboard and campus entirely, but their role shrinks. Maintenance doses preserve neuromuscular adaptations while freeing energy for on-the-wall performance. Don't skip warm-ups or primary strength touch-points entirely.

## Session intent

Each session should have a clear performance goal: send a benchmark, stick a crux move type, or execute a linked sequence cleanly. Log attempts honestly — send rate only improves when rest and intent stay high.
