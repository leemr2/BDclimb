## Power vs strength

Strength is how much force you can produce; power is how quickly you can produce it. Dynamic moves, deadpoints, and snappy lock-offs depend on rate-of-force development (RFD). Mesocycle 2 trains that quality on top of the max strength you built in Mesocycle 1.

## Campus board and limit bouldering

Campus sessions develop explosive finger force through dynamic contacts. Limit bouldering complements this by applying power to real movement on the wall. Together they bridge gym training and performance on hard problems.

## RPE targets this mesocycle

Sessions should feel hard — typically RPE 8–9 on primary work. That reflects higher neural demand from dynamic efforts. Warm-ups and cooldowns stay moderate; the peak effort is concentrated in campus sets and limit attempts.

## What changes from Mesocycle 1

Max hang volume decreases relative to Mesocycle 1 while dynamic work increases. Rest periods stay generous — power quality drops sharply when fatigued. If finger status rises or power feels flat, prioritize recovery over completing every rep.
