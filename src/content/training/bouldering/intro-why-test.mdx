## Why baseline testing matters

Before prescribing loads and progressions, we need to know where you are today. A structured baseline prevents guesswork and reduces injury risk from starting too heavy or too light.

## What Week 0 tests

Your baseline assessment includes a max hang test on a standard edge, campus board reach and power measures, and benchmark boulder attempts. Together these paint a picture of your current finger strength, power, and on-the-wall ability.

## How results drive your program

Max hang results set your target loads for hangboard sessions throughout the program. Campus reach establishes your starting rung. Benchmark boulder send rates become the reference point for tracking climbing performance over 12 weeks.

## What to expect

Testing takes one session and should feel challenging but not maximal to the point of pain. Take full rest between attempts, use consistent grip positions, and note how each effort feels — this context helps interpret retests at Weeks 4, 8, and 12.
