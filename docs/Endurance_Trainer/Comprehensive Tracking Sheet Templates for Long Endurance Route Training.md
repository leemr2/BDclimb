# Comprehensive Tracking Sheet Templates for Long Endurance Route Training

These templates work for 2-day, 3-day, and 4-day training plans. They're designed to be simple enough to maintain consistently while capturing the essential data the research identifies as important for progression and injury prevention.

## How to Use These Templates

### For 2-Day Plan:

- Use Templates 1 (baseline), 2 (weekly), 3A/3B/3C/3D (as relevant), 4 (testing), 5 (monthly)
- Focus on Template 2 weekly summary—critical for managing limited frequency
- Use daily quick-log (Template 6) on rest days to track recovery

### For 3-Day Plan:

- Same as 2-day, all templates apply
- Weekly session log (Template 2) becomes primary tool
- Detailed workout logs (3A-3D) optional but recommended for key sessions

### For 4-Day Plan:

- ALL templates highly recommended due to higher volume
- Template 2 (weekly log) is essential for managing 4-session load
- Template 3 detailed logs help optimize each session type
- Template 5 (monthly dashboard) critical for catching overtraining trends

### Minimum Viable Tracking (if time-constrained):

1. Template 1: Baseline assessment (Week 0 only)
2. Template 2: Weekly session log (every week—NON-NEGOTIABLE)
3. Template 4: Progress testing (Weeks 4, 8, 12)
4. Template 6: Daily quick-log for recovery monitoring

**Research basis:** The training load monitoring literature shows that session-RPE is a valid, practical internal load measure. The injury prevention research emphasizes load management and symptom tracking as modifiable risk factors. These templates capture exactly what the research identifies as important while remaining practical for real-world use.
