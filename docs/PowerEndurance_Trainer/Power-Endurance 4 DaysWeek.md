# 12-Week Power-Endurance Training Plan (Goal 4) - 4 Days/Week

## Routes with Hard Moves, Endurance Demands, and Late Cruxes

------

## Overview

This plan targets routes that require sustained climbing for several minutes while maintaining enough strength and power to execute a difficult crux sequence near the end when you're already fatigued. With **4 dedicated training days per week**, you have optimal frequency to:

1. Separate different training stimuli for better quality and recovery
2. Distribute volume across more sessions (reducing per-session fatigue)
3. Include dedicated technique/skill days
4. Progress faster while managing injury risk better than 3-day schedules

**Key advantage over 3-day plan**: You can separate high-intensity strength work from high-intensity endurance work, allowing better recovery between similar stimuli and potentially 15-25% faster progression.

------

## Step 0: Profile Score Onboarding

**Complete this before the Week 0 assessment.** The Profile Score determines *how fast* your program advances; the assessment battery determines *where it starts*.

Complete the four onboarding inputs from the **CruxTracker Profile Score System** (`docs/CruxTracker_Profile_Score_System.md`):

1. **Climbing age** (C1, max 45 pts): years of consistent climbing at ≥2 sessions/week for ≥8 months/year
2. **Chronological age** (C2, max 25 pts): recovery capacity and collagen synthesis rate
3. **Structured training history** (C3, max 30 pts): exposure to deliberate tendon-loading protocols
4. **Injury ceiling**: finger injury history caps the Final Profile Score regardless of raw score

```
Raw Score = C1 + C2 + C3
Final Profile Score (FPS) = min(Raw Score, Injury Ceiling)
```

| FPS | Tier | Label |
|---|---|---|
| 0–20 | 1 | Developing |
| 21–40 | 2 | Building |
| 41–60 | 3 | Established |
| 61–80 | 4 | Experienced |
| 81–100 | 5 | Seasoned |

**Record your tier before reading further.** Every progression parameter, deload cut, and safety threshold in this plan is tier-keyed. See the quick reference at the bottom of this plan.

> **Tier 1-2 athletes on the 4-day plan**: The Monday/Thursday layout provides a 3-day gap between high-intensity finger sessions, which satisfies Tier 3-5. However, Tier 1-2 athletes are recommended to use a **Tuesday/Saturday layout** (4-day gap) as an additional safety margin. The weekly sRPE target of 1,000-1,400 stated in this plan's Quick Reference Card is appropriate for Tiers 4-5 — Tier 3 athletes on the 4-day plan will approach or reach their ceiling (1,100) by Mesocycle 2 and should monitor closely. Tiers 1-2 should consider whether the 3-day plan better matches their structural readiness.

> **40+ athletes**: Your C2 score already reduces your FPS. If this brings you to Tier 3 or below, strongly consider the 3-day plan as your primary option.

## Pre-Plan Assessment (Week 0)

### Assessment Battery

**1. Finger Maximum Strength**

- **Test**: 7-second maximum hang on 20-22mm edge (half-crimp grip)
- **Protocol**: Warm up thoroughly; add/remove weight in ~5kg increments; 2 min rest between attempts
- **Record**: Maximum load held (bodyweight ± added/removed weight)

**2. Intermittent Finger Endurance**

- Intermittent hangs at 60% of max hang weight; 7 s on / 2 s off; until form breaks or force drops >10%
- **Record**: Total repetitions or total time

**3. Crux-After-Fatigue Simulation**

- Entry section: a defined number of moves at a defined route grade → immediately attempt a crux boulder (6-12 moves); rest 10-15 min; repeat 2-3 times
- **Record**: Entry moves + grade (→ ELS), crux grade + moves completed (→ CDS), round score (ELS + CDS), session total. Also record success rate and average pump before crux. This establishes your Week 0 CAF score baseline.

**4. Session Baseline**: Duration × RPE = sRPE

**5. Injury Screening**: Finger pain (0-10), morning stiffness, shoulder/elbow symptoms, injury history

## Mesocycle 1: Weeks 1-3

### Build Aerobic Base + Establish Max Force

------

### Workout A (Monday): Maximum Finger Strength + Pulling + Antagonist

Come in completely fresh. This is the highest-intensity session of the week.

#### Part 1: Maximum Hangs (35-40 min)

**Structure**:

- Warm up: 10-15 min easy climbing + progressive hangs (50% → 70% → 85%)
- 6 sets × 10 seconds at 85-95% of max hang; 2-3 min rest between sets
- **Week 1**: 6 × 10s at 85% | **Week 2**: 6 × 10s at 88-90% | **Week 3**: 6 × 10s at 90-93%

**Progression Rule**: Governed by your Profile Score tier. After completing the required consecutive confirmation sessions (Tiers 1-2: 3 sessions; Tiers 3-5: 2 sessions) at RPE below your hold threshold, add **your tier's load increment percentage** (e.g., Tier 4 = +4.5% of current load, rounded to nearest 0.25 kg). Never progress if any rep was questionable, and never advance if the minimum time gate has not elapsed. Never progress if RPE meets or exceeds your tier's hold threshold.

**Track**: Load used, all reps completed (yes/no), form quality, finger pain (0-10), session RPE

#### Part 2: Heavy Pulling Strength (20-25 min)

- 10 min rest after hangs
- Weighted pull-ups: 4 sets × 3-5 reps at heavy load; 3 min rest between sets
- **Week 1**: Find working weight for 5 reps | **Week 2**: Same weight, all sets for 5 | **Week 3**: Add 2-5 lbs if Week 2 was solid

**Track**: Load, reps per set, form quality

#### Part 3: Antagonist + Shoulder-Stability Circuit (15 min)

This is a permanent fixture of Workout A across all three mesocycles. It addresses shoulder rotator imbalances and is the primary structural injury-prevention tool in the plan.

**Circuit (2 rounds)**:
- Scapular push-ups: 8-12
- Band external rotations: 12-20 each side
- Face pulls or reverse fly: 10-15
- Push-ups: 8-12
- Y-T-W raises: 6-10 each
- Hollow hold 20-40 s + side plank 20-40 s each side

**Track**: Loads used, shoulder symptom score (0-10)

**Total Session Duration**: 70-80 minutes

------

### Workout B (Tuesday): Aerobic Base + Technique

This is your lowest-intensity session—it should feel easy and help you recover from Monday while building aerobic capacity and movement economy.

#### Full Session: ARC-Style Continuous Climbing with Silent Feet + Fluency Constraints

**Structure**:

- Warm up: 10 min very easy climbing
- **Main work**: 2-3 sets of 15-20 min continuous climbing; rest 5-8 min between sets
- Intensity: RPE 4-6 (light pump, conversational, sustainable)
- **Silent feet constraint**: every foot placement must be silent and "one-and-done" (no scraping or readjusting). Apply during all sets. If you catch yourself scraping, mark it.
- **Fluency constraint**: you may not stop moving for more than 2 seconds unless at a designated rest hold. Log stop count and location per set.
- Both constraints together turn low-intensity mileage into active movement skill training with zero added fatigue cost.
- **Week 1**: 2 × 15 min | **Week 2**: 2 × 18 min | **Week 3**: 3 × 15 min

**Track**: Total time climbed, average RPE per set, pump level (1-10, should stay at 3-5), silent foot slip count, fluency stop count, movement quality notes

**Total Session Duration**: 50-80 minutes

**Why Both Constraints**: Silent feet builds foot precision and reduces wasted energy from repeated repositioning. Fluency laps build continuous movement patterns and reduce micro-rests that cost time and pump. Both transfer directly to route pacing without adding fatigue.

------

### Workout C (Thursday): Bouldering 4×4 + Power-Endurance Intervals

Mesocycle 1 Workout C pairs the 4×4 (anaerobic capacity builder) with interval work (repeatability under incomplete rest). Do 4×4 first while fresher.

#### Part 1: Bouldering 4×4 (35-45 min)

**Structure**:

- Pick **4 boulder problems** 2-4 grades below your limit (pumpy when fresh, doable)
- Climb each problem **4 times** with minimal rest between climbs (step off, chalk, go)
- After all 4 problems × 4 reps (16 total), rest **10-12 minutes**
- **Intensity**: RPE 8-9; late reps shaky but not failing every time
- **Week 1**: 2 rounds | **Week 2**: 2 rounds, slightly harder problems | **Week 3**: 3 rounds

**Track**: Rounds completed at target grade, falls per round, recovery quality between rounds (1-5)

#### Part 2: Route-Style Intervals (25-30 min)

- 10 min rest after 4×4

**Structure**:

- 4-6 repeats of 60-90 second hard climbing; rest 1:1; RPE 8-9; complete 1-2 sets with 8-10 min rest between sets
- **Week 1**: 1 set × 4 × 60s | **Week 2**: 1 set × 5 × 75s | **Week 3**: 2 sets × 4 × 60s

**Track**: Intervals completed at target intensity, quality through all reps, pump level at end of set

#### Part 3: Core Conditioning (15 min)

- Front plank: 3 × 45-60 s
- Hanging knee raises: 3 × 10-12
- Side plank: 2 × 30 s each side

**Total Session Duration**: 70-85 minutes

**Why This Pairing**: 4×4 builds raw anaerobic capacity under accumulating fatigue—the "engine" that keeps you moving before the crux. Intervals build repeatability under incomplete rest. Together they develop the capacity side of PE before the specificity sessions practice the execution side.

------

### Workout D (Saturday): Crux-After-Fatigue Specificity

Your most specific session. 2 rest days beforehand allow full quality.

#### Warm-Up: Silent Feet Primer (10-15 min)

Apply the silent feet rule during the last 5-10 minutes of warm-up. No extra time cost.

#### Full Session: Crux-After-Fatigue Drill

**Structure**:

- 5-6 rounds:
  - Part A (Entry): A defined number of moves at a defined route grade — don't fall, stay controlled
  - Part B (immediately): Attempt a 6-12 move crux sequence (RPE 9)
  - Rest: 10-12 minutes between rounds
- **Week 1**: 5 rounds, ~20 entry moves at 5.9 (ELS ≈ 20)
- **Week 2**: 5 rounds, ~25 entry moves at 5.9, or same moves at 5.10a (ELS ≈ 25–27.5)
- **Week 3**: 6 rounds, ~30 entry moves at 5.9, or ~25 at 5.10a (ELS ≈ 27.5–30)

**Track per round**: Entry grade + moves → ELS; crux grade + moves completed → CDS; round score (ELS + CDS). Session total = sum of all round scores (5–6 rounds). Also log pump before crux (1–10), execution quality (1–5), pacing notes, mental state.

**Total Session Duration**: 75-95 minutes

------

### Week 4: Deload + Retest

Reduce volume by ~40-50% across all sessions.

**Monday**: 4 × 10s hangs at 85%, 3 × 3 pull-ups, antagonist circuit (1 round). ~50 min.

**Tuesday**: 1 × 20 min ARC with silent feet + fluency constraints. ~35 min.

**Thursday**: Skip 4×4. 1 set × 4 × 60s intervals. Core work. ~45 min.

**Saturday (Testing Day)**: Skip Workout D. Complete full assessment battery (max hang, intermittent endurance, crux-after-fatigue). ~90-120 min.

**Track all test results; compare to Week 0; adjust Mesocycle 2 loads accordingly.**

## Mesocycle 2: Weeks 5-7

### Power-Endurance Build + Increased Specificity

------

### Workout A (Monday): Strength Maintenance + Campus/Power Work + Antagonist

#### Part 1: Maintenance Max Hangs (25-30 min)

- 4-5 sets × 10 seconds at 85-90% of updated max (from Week 4 test); 2-3 min rest
- Maintain quality—this is not a strength-building phase

#### Part 2: Campus Board or Explosive Work (25-30 min)

- 10 min rest after hangs
- Choose 2 campus exercises per session:
  - Max reach ladders (1-4-7 or similar)
  - Controlled doubles (1-3-5-7)
  - Max reach singles (find your limit)
- 4 sets of each exercise; 2-3 min rest; maximum velocity and power
- **Week 5**: 25mm rungs, learn movement | **Week 6**: refine technique | **Week 7**: progress to 20mm if ready

**If campus board unavailable**: substitute dynamic limit-move bouldering with explosive elements.

**Track**: Highest rung reached, total moves completed, quality of execution, finger/shoulder pain

#### Part 3: Antagonist + Shoulder-Stability Circuit (15 min)

Same 2-round circuit as Mesocycle 1. Non-negotiable.

**Total Session Duration**: 65-75 min

------

### Workout B (Tuesday): Continued Aerobic Base with Technique Constraints

Same ARC format as Mesocycle 1—maintain the aerobic foundation.

**Structure**:

- Silent feet + fluency constraints applied to all sets
- **Week 5**: 3 × 15 min | **Week 6**: 2 × 22 min | **Week 7**: 3 × 18 min

**Track**: Total time climbed, pump levels (should stay low even as duration increases), silent foot slip count, fluency stop count

**Do not let this session become hard.** Its value depends on staying at RPE 4-6.

------

### Workout C (Thursday): Intermittent Finger Endurance OR Critical-Force Blocks + Light Bouldering

#### Part 1: Intermittent Hangs at 60% MVC OR Critical-Force Blocks (alternating weeks)

Alternate between these two. They target the same energy system from different angles and prevent accommodation.

**Option A — Intermittent Hangs at 60% MVC (Weeks 5 and 7)**:

- 7 seconds on, 3 seconds off
- 4-6 sets to near-failure; 3 min rest between sets
- **Week 5**: 4 sets | **Week 7**: 6 sets

**Track**: Reps per set, total time under tension, force consistency, finger pain (0-10)

**Option B — Critical-Force Blocks (Week 6)**:

- Rhythm: 7 s on / 3 s off
- Target: slightly below your all-out end-test intensity—RPE 8-9, stable and consistent (not maximal effort)
- **2-3 blocks of 2 minutes** at this intensity
- **6-8 minutes full rest** between blocks
- Benchmark session: log meticulously and compare across cycles

**Track**: Reps within each block at consistent form, force stability late in each block, recovery time required

**Why Alternate**: Intermittent hangs at 60% MVC build rhythmic endurance. Critical-force blocks train the sustainability boundary—the intensity above which fatigue accumulates non-linearly. Rotating prevents accommodation and gives you an objective intensity anchor to track progress.

#### Part 2: Light Bouldering (20-25 min)

- Easy to moderate boulders (RPE 5-6); 8-12 problems
- Focus: technique, precision, efficiency—not fatigue
- **Silent feet rule active throughout**

**Total Session Duration**: 65-80 min

------

### Workout D (Saturday): Enhanced Crux-After-Fatigue

**Structure**:

- 5-6 rounds; ELS increases; crux grade rises
- **Week 5**: 5 rounds — increase ELS to ~35 (e.g., 30 moves at 5.9 or 25 at 5.10b); crux bumped +1 grade
- **Week 6**: 5 rounds — maintain ELS from Week 5; maintain crux grade
- **Week 7**: 6 rounds — increase ELS further (e.g., 35 moves at 5.9 or 25 at 5.10c); maintain crux grade

**Additional Focus**:
- Practice efficient shaking during the entry section
- Experiment with pacing (faster vs. slower)
- Try different rest positions
- Mental rehearsal before each crux attempt

**Track per round**: Entry grade + moves → ELS; crux grade + moves completed → CDS; round score. Session total. Session scores should climb vs. Mesocycle 1 despite harder entry and crux — this is the primary progress signal. Also track quality rating (1–5), pump management, pacing notes, mental state.

**Total Session Duration**: 85-105 min

------

### Week 8: Deload + Retest

40-50% volume reduction across all sessions.

**Monday**: 3 × 10s hangs at 85%, 2 sets campus work (instead of 4), antagonist circuit (1 round).

**Tuesday**: 1 × 20 min ARC with silent feet + fluency constraints.

**Thursday**: 3 sets intermittent hangs (skip critical-force blocks), light bouldering with silent feet.

**Saturday**: **Testing Day** (not Workout D). Full assessment battery.

**Week 8 Review**: Track Week 0 → Week 4 → Week 8 trajectory. Adjust all loads for Mesocycle 3.

## Mesocycle 3: Weeks 9-11

### Specific Linking + Redpoint Focus

------

### Workout A (Monday): Minimal Strength Maintenance + Route Attempts

#### Part 1: Maintenance Hangs (20-25 min)

- 3-4 sets × 10 seconds at 85-90%; 3 min rest; focus on perfect form

#### Part 2: Route Attempts (40-50 min)

- 3-4 burns on goal route or similar difficulty
- Full rest between burns (15-20 min)
- Apply the **fluency constraint** on rehearsal burns (log stops >2 seconds). Drop constraint on genuine send attempts.
- **Week 9**: Learn beta, find rests | **Week 10**: Link sections | **Week 11**: Go for sends

**Track**: High point per burn, falls/rests, stop count on rehearsal burns, energy at crux (1-10), beta refinements, quality of execution

**Total Session Duration**: 60-75 min

------

### Workout B (Tuesday): Active Recovery + Technique

**Structure**:

- 30-40 min very easy climbing (RPE 3-4)
- **Silent feet constraint active throughout**
- Focus areas: footwork precision, hip positioning, efficient shake technique, breathing patterns
- Optional: 15 min mobility/flexibility work

**Total Session Duration**: 45-60 min. Keep this easy—you're managing cumulative fatigue from route attempts.

------

### Workout C (Thursday): Threshold Intervals + Mental Practice

#### Part 1: Sustained Hard Climbing (50-60 min)

- 4-6 repeats of 2-4 minute threshold efforts; 4-6 min rest between repeats; RPE 7-8
- Practice pacing, shake timing, clip positioning
- **Week 9**: 4 × 2 min | **Week 10**: 5 × 3 min | **Week 11**: 4 × 4 min (reduce volume before taper)

**Track**: Time at intensity, pacing consistency, shake effectiveness, technical observations

#### Part 2: Mental Practice (10 min)

- Visualization of goal route
- Breathing exercises
- Mental rehearsal of crux execution under fatigue

**Total Session Duration**: 60-70 min

------

### Workout D (Saturday): Performance Simulation

**Structure**:

- 4-5 full route attempts on goal route or similar difficulty
- Full rest between attempts (15-25 min)
- Treat each like a genuine redpoint attempt
- **Week 9**: 5 attempts, learning mode | **Week 10**: 4 attempts, execution mode | **Week 11**: 4 attempts, sending mode

**Track**: High point per attempt, falls/rests (where and why), crux execution quality, energy at crux (1-10), beta refinements, mental state

**Total Session Duration**: 90-120 min

## Progression Rules

### For Hangboard (Workout A)

**Mesocycle 1**: Progress is governed by your **Profile Score tier** (see Step 0 and quick reference below). Replace the old "+2-3kg" rule:
- Add **your tier's load increment percentage** (Tier 1: 1.5%, Tier 2: 2.5%, Tier 3: 3.5%, Tier 4: 4.5%, Tier 5: 5.0%) of the current working load, rounded to nearest 0.25 kg.
- Wait for **your tier's sessions-to-confirm** (Tiers 1-2: 3 sessions; Tiers 3-5: 2 sessions) AND the minimum calendar time per step.
- **Hold gate**: if session RPE ≥ your tier's hold threshold, reset the counter.
- **Regression gate**: if session RPE exceeds the regression threshold for 2 consecutive sessions, drop one step.

**Mesocycles 2-3**: Maintain load or add very conservatively. Priority has shifted to other qualities. Even maintenance sessions are subject to the hold and regression gates.

### For Aerobic Work (Workout B)

**Mesocycle 1**: Increase duration by 2-3 minutes per week or add one set. Keep intensity LOW (RPE 4-6).

**Mesocycles 2-3**: Maintain duration achieved in Mesocycle 1. May slightly increase to RPE 6-7 max.

### For 4×4 (Workout C, Meso 1)

Progress round count (2 → 3) before raising problem difficulty. Only increase difficulty after 3 clean rounds for 2 sessions.

### For Critical-Force Blocks (Workout C, Meso 2)

Increase target force only once all blocks are completed with stable technique. Cap at 2-3 blocks.

### For Power-Endurance (Workout C)

Progress priorities (one at a time):
1. Add reps/duration within sets (by your tier's volume increment: 10-20%)
2. Add sets
3. Reduce rest by your tier's rest reduction step (Tier 1: 15 sec; Tier 5: 30-45 sec) — never in max-strength sessions
4. Increase difficulty

Never progress if success rate drops below 30% for 2 consecutive weeks. Confirmation sessions before each step: Tiers 1-2 require 3 sessions; Tiers 3-5 require 2 sessions.

### For Specific Work (Workout D)

Change only one variable per step (confirm for required sessions before advancing):
1. ELS — add entry moves or raise entry grade (primary progression lever)
2. Number of rounds (4 → 5 → 6)
3. Crux grade (+1 grade when session score plateaus AND success rate >60%)
4. Rest reduction (only in final mesocycle; by your tier's rest reduction step)

## Safety Guidelines

**Session Duration Caps**:
- Workout A: max 90 min
- Workout B: max 90 min
- Workout C: max 80 min
- Workout D: max 120 min

**Sequencing**: Never place high-intensity finger sessions back-to-back. Monday and Thursday are separated by 3 days; if schedule shifts, maintain 2+ day gap between finger-intensive sessions.

**Early deload triggers (tier-specific)** — these trigger an unscheduled deload regardless of calendar position:
- **Tier 1**: any A2 or pulley-region pain during or after climbing
- **Tier 2**: morning stiffness in finger joints on 2 or more consecutive days
- **Tier 3**: morning stiffness in finger joints on 3 or more consecutive days
- **Tiers 4-5**: standard red flags only (sharp pain, pop, significant swelling)

**Deloads (Weeks 4 & 8)**: Mandatory. With 4 days/week, accumulated fatigue is real. Apply your tier's volume reduction (Tier 1: 55%; Tier 5: 35%). Tiers 1-2 also reduce working load by 15% and 10% respectively; Tiers 3-5 maintain intensity. Skipping deloads substantially raises injury risk.

**Mid-cycle injury**: Apply the injury ceiling from the Profile Score System. If the ceiling drops your FPS into a lower tier, that tier's parameters (load increment, confirmation sessions, RPE gates, minimum time) take effect immediately at the next session. Current load is reduced to the new tier's floor if it exceeds the new ceiling.

**Red Flags**:

| Red Flag | Likely Cause | Immediate Action |
| -------- | ------------ | ---------------- |
| Persistent fatigue despite adequate sleep | Volume too high | Insert extra rest day |
| Pain in multiple finger joints | Overuse across too many sessions | Full week off finger intensity |
| Performance declining 2+ weeks | Inadequate recovery | Extend deload to 2 weeks |
| Dreading sessions | Mental/physical overtraining | Drop to 3 days/week for 2 weeks |
| Elevated resting heart rate (+10 bpm) | Systemic overload | Complete rest 3-5 days |

**Warm-up is mandatory** before any finger intensity. Easy to moderate climbing must precede heavy work.

### The Most Common Mistake

Turning Tuesday (Workout B) into a hard session. It must stay at RPE 4-6. The plan's training quality depends on Workout B being genuinely easy.
