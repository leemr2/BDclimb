# 12-Week Power-Endurance Training Plan - 3 Days/Week

## Routes with Hard Moves, Endurance Demands, and Late Cruxes

------

## Overview

This plan is designed for routes that require you to manage sustained climbing for several minutes while maintaining enough strength and power to execute a difficult crux sequence near the end when you're already fatigued. Think of routes where you might be climbing at a moderate intensity for 3-5 minutes, then suddenly encountering a V5-V7 boulder problem right before the chains.

The training philosophy combines three elements:

1. **Aerobic base** — so you can recover between moves and maintain efficiency
2. **Anaerobic capacity** — for sustained hard sequences and the crux itself
3. **Strength under fatigue** — practicing hard moves when you're already pumped

------

## Pre-Plan Assessment (Week 0)

Before starting, establish baselines across several domains. These tests will be repeated at Weeks 4, 8, and 12.

### Assessment Battery

**1. Finger Maximum Strength**

- **Test**: 7-second maximum hang on 20-22mm edge (half-crimp grip)
- **Protocol**: Warm up thoroughly; add/remove weight in ~5kg increments; 2 min rest between attempts; stop when you cannot complete 7 seconds
- **Record**: Maximum load held (bodyweight ± added/removed weight)

**2. Intermittent Finger Endurance**

- **Test**: Intermittent hangs at 60% of your max hang weight; 7 s on / 2 s off until form breaks or force drops >10%
- **Record**: Total repetitions or total time

**3. Crux-After-Fatigue Simulation**

- Entry section: a defined number of moves at a defined route grade → immediately attempt a crux boulder (6-12 moves); rest 10-15 min; repeat 2-3 times
- **Record**: Entry moves + grade (→ ELS), crux grade + moves completed (→ CDS), round score (ELS + CDS), session total. Also record success rate and average pump before crux. This establishes your Week 0 CAF score baseline.

**4. Session Baseline**

- Duration × RPE = sRPE (your load monitoring metric throughout the plan)

**5. Injury Screening**

- Finger pain (location, 0-10), morning stiffness, shoulder/elbow discomfort, previous injuries

------

## The 12-Week Structure

```
Weeks 1-3: Aerobic Base + Max Force Foundation
Week 4: Deload + Test
Weeks 5-7: Power-Endurance Build + Crux Simulations
Week 8: Deload + Test
Weeks 9-11: Specific Linking + Redpoint Focus
Week 12: Taper + Performance Week
```

## Mesocycle 1: Weeks 1-3

### Build Aerobic Base + Establish Max Force

------

### Workout A: Long Intervals with Fluency Constraint (Monday)

**Structure**:

- 4 sets of 6-8 minute continuous climbing
- Rest 4-5 minutes between sets
- Intensity: RPE 6-7 (mild pump, conversation possible)
- Use traversing, circuits, or up-down routes
- **Fluency constraint**: apply a no-stop rule (>2 seconds stationary is a logged stop) to every set. Record total stops per set and where they occur (which move, which clip). Aim to reduce stop count week over week. This builds movement economy at submaximal intensity—the same quality that lets you conserve energy before the late crux.
- **Week 1**: 4 × 6 min | **Week 2**: 4 × 7 min | **Week 3**: 4 × 8 min

**Track**: Total time climbed, average RPE per set, pump level (1-10), stop count per set, next-morning finger stiffness, sRPE

------

### Workout B: Bouldering 4×4 + Crux-After-Fatigue Drill (Friday)

Workout B in Mesocycle 1 pairs the 4×4 (anaerobic capacity under accumulating fatigue) with the crux simulation (specific execution skill). Do 4×4 first while you're fresher; it feeds directly into the simulation.

#### Warm-Up: Silent Feet Primer (10-15 min)

During the last 5-10 minutes of warm-up, impose the silent feet rule: every foot placement must be silent and "one-and-done" (no scraping). No extra time cost; reinforces precision before intensity begins.

#### Part 1: Bouldering 4×4 (35-40 min)

**Structure**:

- Pick **4 boulder problems** 2-4 grades below your limit (pumpy when fresh, not failing constantly)
- Climb each problem **4 times** with minimal rest between climbs (step off, chalk, go)
- After all 4 problems × 4 reps (16 total), rest **10-12 minutes**
- **Intensity**: RPE 8-9; late reps should be shaky but not failing every time
- **Week 1**: 2 rounds | **Week 2**: 2 rounds, slightly harder problems | **Week 3**: 3 rounds

**Track**: Rounds completed at target grade, falls per round, recovery quality between rounds (1-5)

#### Part 2: Crux-After-Fatigue Drill (40-50 min)

- Rest 10-12 minutes after 4×4

**Structure**:

- Part A (Entry): A defined number of moves at a defined route grade — don't fall, stay controlled
- Part B (immediately): Attempt a 6-12 move crux sequence (RPE 9)
- Rest 10-12 minutes between rounds
- **Week 1**: 3 rounds, ~20 entry moves at 5.9 (ELS ≈ 20)
- **Week 2**: 3 rounds, ~25 entry moves at 5.9, or same moves at 5.10a (ELS ≈ 25–27.5)
- **Week 3**: 3 rounds, ~30 entry moves at 5.9, or ~25 at 5.10a (ELS ≈ 27.5–30)

**Track per round**: Entry grade + moves → ELS; crux grade + moves completed → CDS; round score (ELS + CDS). Session total = sum of round scores. Also log success/fail, pump before crux (1–10), execution quality (1–5), sRPE.

**Total Session Duration**: 90-110 minutes

**Why This Pairing**: 4×4 builds raw anaerobic capacity. The crux simulation develops the specific skill of executing hard moves when already pumped. Together they cover the "can sustain" and "can execute" dimensions of PE.

------

### Workout C: Maximum Hangs + Antagonist Circuit (Wednesday)

#### Part 1: Maximum Hangs (30-35 min)

**Structure**:

- 6 sets × 10 seconds at 85-95% of max hang; 2 min rest between sets
- **Week 1**: 6 × 10s at 85% | **Week 2**: 6 × 10s at 87-90% | **Week 3**: 6 × 10s at 90-92%

**Progression Rule**: If you complete all 6 reps with good form for 2 consecutive sessions, add 2-3kg. Never add more than 5% per week.

**Track**: Load used, all reps completed (yes/no), form quality, finger pain (0-10), sRPE

#### Part 2: Antagonist + Shoulder-Stability Circuit (20-25 min)

This is a permanent fixture of Workout C across all three mesocycles. It addresses the shoulder rotator imbalances common in climbers and is the primary injury-prevention tool in the plan.

**Circuit (2-3 rounds)**:
- Scapular push-ups: 8-12
- Band external rotations: 12-20 each side
- Face pulls or reverse fly: 10-15
- Push-ups: 8-12
- Y-T-W raises: 6-10 each
- Hollow hold 20-40 s + side plank 20-40 s each side

**Track**: Loads used, shoulder symptom score (0-10)

**Total Session Duration**: 55-65 minutes

------

### Week 4: Deload + Retest

- Reduce total volume by 50%; maintain intensity
- **Monday**: 2 × 6 min intervals with fluency constraint (instead of 4 × 8)
- **Wednesday**: 4 × 10s hangs at 85% + 1 round antagonist circuit
- **Friday**: Skip 4×4; 2 rounds crux-after-fatigue
- **Testing (Friday or add a day)**: max hang, intermittent endurance, crux-after-fatigue benchmark

------

## Mesocycle 2: Weeks 5-7

### Power-Endurance Build + Threshold Work

------

### Workout A: Intermittent Finger Endurance OR Critical-Force Blocks (Monday)

Alternate between these two across the mesocycle. They target the same energy system from different angles.

**Option A — Intermittent Hangs at 60% MVC (Weeks 5 and 7)**:

- 7 seconds on, 3 seconds off
- 3-5 sets to near-failure (stop before form breaks); 2-3 min rest between sets
- **Week 5**: 3 sets | **Week 7**: 5 sets

**Track**: Repetitions per set, total time under tension, force consistency, finger pain (0-10), sRPE

**Option B — Critical-Force Blocks (Week 6)**:

- Rhythm: 7 s on / 3 s off
- Target intensity: slightly below what you'd hit at the end of an all-out test—RPE 8-9, stable and consistent across the block (not maximal, not grinding)
- **2-3 blocks of 2 minutes** at this intensity
- **6-8 minutes full rest** between blocks
- Treat this as a benchmark session: log meticulously and compare across cycles

**Track**: Reps completed within each block at consistent form, force stability late in each block, recovery time required

**Why Alternate**: Intermittent hangs at 60% MVC build rhythmic endurance. Critical-force blocks train the intensity boundary between sustainable and non-sustainable output. Rotating prevents accommodation and provides an objective intensity anchor.

------

### Workout B: Crux-After-Fatigue Progression (Friday)

Same format as Mesocycle 1, increasing ELS and crux grade:
- **Week 5**: 4 rounds — increase ELS to ~35 (e.g., 30 moves at 5.9 or 25 at 5.10b); crux bumped +1 grade
- **Week 6**: 4 rounds — maintain ELS from Week 5; maintain crux grade
- **Week 7**: 5 rounds — increase ELS further (e.g., 35 moves at 5.9 or 25 at 5.10c); maintain crux grade

**Track per round**: Entry grade + moves → ELS; crux grade + moves completed → CDS; round score. Session total. Compare session scores to Mesocycle 1 — scores should rise as both entry load increases and crux execution improves. Also track quality under fatigue, shake efficiency, sRPE.

------

### Workout C: Strength Maintenance + Antagonist Circuit (Wednesday)

**Max hangs** reduced to 4 sets × 10 seconds at 85-90% (down from 6 sets). Antagonist circuit: same 2-3 rounds.

**Why reduced**: Strength is being maintained, not built—priority has shifted to endurance. Less volume preserves the adaptation from Mesocycle 1 while managing overall fatigue.

------

### Week 8: Deload + Retest

- 50% volume reduction; maintain intensity
- **Monday**: 2 sets intermittent hangs (skip critical-force blocks)
- **Wednesday**: 3 × 10s hangs at 85% + 1 round antagonist circuit
- **Friday**: 2-3 rounds crux-after-fatigue
- **Testing**: Full battery. Compare to Week 4 and Week 0.

------

## Mesocycle 3: Weeks 9-11

### Specific Linking + Redpoint Practice

------

### Workout A: Route Linking Practice (Monday)

**Structure**:

- 3-5 high-quality burns on routes similar to your goal
- Full rest (10-20 minutes) between attempts
- Apply the **fluency constraint** on beta rehearsal burns: log stops >2 seconds and where they occur. On genuine send attempts, drop the constraint—just climb.
- **Week 9**: 3 burns, lower section efficiency | **Week 10**: 4 burns, full links with rest | **Week 11**: 4-5 burns, prioritize sending

**Track**: High point per burn, falls/rests, pump level at crux (1-10), stop count on rehearsal burns, tactical notes, sRPE

------

### Workout B: Threshold Intervals (Friday)

**Structure**:

- 6-8 repeats × 60-90 seconds hard climbing; rest 1:1 (60-90 seconds); RPE 8-9
- **Week 9**: 6 × 60s | **Week 10**: 7 × 75s | **Week 11**: 6 × 90s (reduce volume before taper)

**Track**: Intervals completed, intensity maintained (yes/no), power feel, sRPE

------

### Workout C: Strength Maintenance + Antagonist Circuit (Wednesday)

- 4 sets × 10 seconds at 85-90%; 2 min rest. Antagonist circuit: 2-3 rounds (same as prior mesocycles).

------

## Week 12: Taper + Performance

- **Monday**: 2 easy burns on goal route, technique focus only
- **Wednesday**: 3 × 10s hangs at 85%, very light. Antagonist circuit: 1 round.
- **Thursday-Friday**: Rest
- **Saturday**: Performance attempt(s) on goal route
- **Sunday**: Final assessment if not completed Saturday

## Progression Rules

**For Hangboard**: Add load only when 2 consecutive sessions are completed with good form. Increase by 2-3kg maximum. Never progress both intensity AND volume in the same week.

**For 4×4**: Progress round count (2 → 3) before raising problem difficulty.

**For Critical-Force Blocks**: Increase target force only once all blocks are stable. Cap at 2-3 blocks.

**For Intervals/Endurance**: Add time/reps (volume) first; then reduce rest slightly; then increase difficulty. Progress only ONE variable every 2 weeks.

**For Crux-After-Fatigue**: First increase ELS (more entry moves or higher entry grade); then round count; then crux grade. Only raise crux grade when session score plateaus and success rate exceeds 60%. Change one variable per week. Quality > quantity always.

------

## Safety Guidelines

**Stop immediately if**: sharp pain or "pop" in finger; pain increasing during session; swelling in finger joints; pain worse next morning.

**Reduce volume/intensity if**: morning finger stiffness lasting >30 minutes for 3+ consecutive days; sRPE monotony (same high load week after week); skin repeatedly splitting; motivation dropping significantly.

**Warm-up**: 10 min easy climbing + progressive hangs (50% → 70% → 85% of working weight). Never start maximum efforts cold.

**Deload Weeks 4 & 8 are mandatory** — injury prevention, not optional. If skipped, overuse risk increases substantially.
